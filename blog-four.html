Website Development:

I am going through different blog websites and analysing how their wireframes/interface impacts my experience with their website. While doing this, I compare my ideas to the website in front of me and I have noticed that the majority of my wireframes are too cluttered; I also noticed that I am trying to put too much content on my homepage which takes away from the rest of the website. I do think that this 'clutter' is down to me just playing around with CSS and figuring out what I can do with the programming language.

The Basic Principles of User Interface Design:

Keeping in mind my above-mentioned challenges when designing my website, I found this guide/tips very helpful. One thing that I do believe is crucial for a website is consistency. Last year when designing games, I made the mistake of assuming my players (users) knew how everything worked so I tried to push more complex mechanics into my games (you can imagine how chaotic that was). I do think that my website needs to allow the user to find out what they need to without having to go through too many complex steps. It is important to note the difference between a design that will be considered 'revolutionary' by the user or if it is just unnecessary.
