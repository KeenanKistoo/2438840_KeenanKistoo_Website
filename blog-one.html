The Library of Babel (1998):

It took me a while to process the reading because of how content-heavy it was but I tried to break the different ideas down. In my attempt to analyze the reading, I tried to find similarities between the library and the internet. There are two that I believe are worth mentioning: 1. the "number of hexagonal galleries" are described as "infinite" (Borges 1998: 112). To search and process that much content will be overwhelming. With the internet, information can be accessed quicker but there are still various websites/pages that can be accessed. Even though information can be gathered more efficiently, the variety is still the same ~ there are valid points but also unnecessary information. 2. Borges discusses the identical layout of each gallery. This makes it easier for people to navigate through and within the galleries. We practice the same ideas when creating websites/applications/etc. in the form of symbols. For instance, the gear cog or magnifying glass represents settings or a search bar respectively.
