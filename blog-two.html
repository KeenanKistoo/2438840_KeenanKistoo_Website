Starting HTML:

Similar to other programming languages, HTML has certain conventions and methods to efficiently write code. I worked with C# last year and for most of the year, I was the only person reading and altering my code. For our final exam, we worked in groups; to ensure that my group members understood my code, I had to practice certain conventions that I previously bypassed. This took longer and became frustrating. I would like to practice the conventions I am taught consistently from the beginning to ensure that I do not repeat my mistakes. I do enjoy HTML and look forward to exploring the language more.

Reflections On The Reading And The Case Study:

I did not realise how sheltered I was when growing up. When I started university, I became aware of screen readers, and several other life-altering scenarios. I also think that because of my cultural background, my peers at the time would feel obligated to hide these life-altering scenarios. I would like to do more research into the different ways that I can improve my code to ensure that people who do face these scenarios can interact with my websites, apps, etc. In terms of the reading, Why Choose HTML5, emphasises certain practices to ensure that websites are accessible to all. It was a very packed reading that had several sections, excuse the pun, that I struggled to grasp but as I become more "fluent" in HTML, I do think that certain practices will become second nature.

Internet Histories:

The growth and development of the internet have always fascinated me. It feels as if one day I stared at a simple HTML document with minimal images and quite a bit of text, and the next day there was this aesthetic masterpiece of a website that had several different capabilities and functionalities. Then again, I remember when I argued that the speed of a 3G network was faster than the speed of light, now I rub my temples in frustration whenever I run a speed test and it is below 8MBps. The timeline illustrates how far the internet has moved/evolved from its initial creation. I look forward to playing role in future developments in websites and the internet as a whole.
